About Me

Hi! I'm David from Philadelphia, PA and I attend the University of Virginia, where I'm pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.A. in Economics. My main extracurricular involvements are in the Virginia Venture Fund and Future Business Leaders of America, where I'm acting as the Director of Programming and Chapter Relations. Recently, I had a great time interning for Blockchain Valley Ventures, a Switzerland-based venture capital company that invests in blockchain-enabled businesses, as well as submitting a research paper to the IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy. This past summer, I worked as a software engineering intern at Embark, a financial consulting firm in Dallas, TX. Next summer, I will be working as a software development engineering intern at Amazon in Seattle, WA. Outside of school, I love playing soccer, biking, cooking, traveling, and learning about different cultures. Feel free to contact me below!


My Projects

Exercisify
This was a fun semester-long class project where I worked in a team to develop a web app that allows users to self-report exercise they've completed, share it and interact with others, and earn rewards while doing so. It uses Django, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Plotly, and Travis CI, and I also served as the testing manager. You can view a demo here.

Web Scraper
Check out this useful web scraper I built in Python using Beautiful Soup to collect a large amount of data from an online medical advice forum. This was one of my super important tasks as a part of doing research at UVA; our paper was submitted to the IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy.

Simplified Investing
I worked in a team to create a web app that uses JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Materialize, Chart.js, and Firebase. Its goal is to showcase the power of compound interest, and it allows users to visualize how money can grow in a variety of different accounts.

Task Manager
I made a cool GUI with Java Swing to manage a list of tasks. Users can add, remove, and search for tasks in their list. The tasks are continuously sorted to be in order (i.e. first morning tasks, then afternoon, then evening, then anytime) for the user's convenience.

Digital Library
I developed this React app that uses the Google Books API along with Express and Firebase to allow users to search for a book, view its relevant details (including the title, author, and description), and add/remove books to/from their personal library.

File Portal
As a part of my internship at Embark, I implemented a file portal with Google Apps Script that allows clients to instantly upload files to Embark's Google Drive and view the names of the files that they have previously uploaded, all without needing a Google account.
